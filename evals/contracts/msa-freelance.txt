MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT — FREELANCE

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") governs all engagements between Orion Media Partners ("Client") and the freelance service provider ("Provider").

1. SERVICES. Provider will perform services described in one or more ordering documents issued by the Client.

2. UNILATERAL AMENDMENT. The Client may modify, amend, or update the terms of this Agreement at any time in its sole discretion by posting updated terms or providing notice, and the Provider's continued performance shall constitute acceptance of the modified terms.

3. PAYMENT. Invoices are payable Net-90. The Client may, at its discretion, set off, deduct, or withhold any amounts it believes are owed or in dispute. No late-payment interest or fees shall accrue against the Client under any circumstances.

4. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY. The Client's total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the lesser of fees paid in the prior thirty (30) days or $100. The Provider's liability shall be unlimited. The Client shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, or consequential damages, while the Provider shall remain fully liable for the same.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY. All work product is assigned to the Client upon creation, regardless of whether the corresponding invoice has been paid.

6. AUTO-RENEWAL & EXCLUSIVITY. This Agreement automatically renews for successive one-year terms unless the Provider gives one hundred twenty (120) days' written notice prior to renewal. During the term and for one year afterward, the Provider shall work exclusively for the Client within the media production field.

7. TERMINATION. The Client may terminate for convenience at any time with no further obligation. Upon any termination, all unpaid invoices for completed work are forfeited at the Client's discretion.

8. WARRANTY & SUPPORT. The Provider shall provide unlimited post-delivery support and corrections at no charge for the lifetime of the work product.

9. GOVERNING LAW & FEES. Disputes shall be resolved in the Client's home jurisdiction, and the prevailing party clause applies only in the Client's favor: the Provider shall pay the Client's legal fees in any dispute, but not vice versa.
